Preparing for Your Smart Meter Upgrade

From 1 December 2025, Diamond Energy will begin upgrading basic electricity meters as part of the Accelerated Smart Meter Rollout Program (ASMROP). If you’re included in the rollout, here’s what you need to know:


Who Is Included in the Rollout?

Customers are selected based on coordination between:

  • Distribution Network Service Providers (DNSPs) – who submit Legacy Meter Replacement Plans (LMRPs) to the Australian Energy Regulator (AER).
  • Diamond Energy – who notifies customers and manages installation logistics.
  • Metering Coordinators (MCs) – who carry out the meter replacements.

How much will a smart meter cost me?

We have developed a special offer for you! Smart meter installation is free of charge for all standard installations Fees may apply for non-standard installations. Where possible, we will advise you in advance if this is the case.


What happens once I agree to get a smart meter?

We will forward your information to a qualified smart meter installer. They will contact you and arrange a time for the meter to be installed. We will then advise you of the confirmed date for the meter installation.


It takes about an hour to install and you will probably be without power during this time.

We will advise you when the installation is complete. The installer needs access to your meter board and if this is easily accessible you don’t even need to be home during the installation.


Will my rates change after a smart meter is installed?

Local poles and wires company are continuing to introduce new rules resulting in amendments to your network charges when you have a smart meter installed. This may result in you no longer being eligible to receive the same rates. Should this be the case, we would apply the applicable time of use rates that correspond with the updated network charges. You can contact us for updated details that are specific to your area.


What changes can I expect, after the meter is installed?

The data provided by the smart meter enables you to:

  • Access your half hourly energy usage so you can better manage and monitor your electricity use and reduce your bills.
  • Have your meter remotely read, reducing the likelihood of estimated bills. Meter data collected remotely negates the need for quarterly meter access.

We can also help you understand your electricity usage better by:

  • Providing useful tips to help you save.
  • Analysing your bill and usage profile to ensure you are on the right tariff structure to achieve maximum savings.

Do I have a smart meter?

Most customers in Victoria already have a smart meter. Some customers in NSW, SA and Qld may also have a smart meter. If you are unsure of what type of meter you have, you can contact us.


Are smart meters safe? Do they cause health issues?

All smart meters must comply with the electromagnetic exposure limits developed by the Australian Radiation Protection and Nuclear Safety Agency. The same limits apply to mobile and cordless phones, Wi-Fi routers and baby monitors.


The Australian Radiation Protection and Nuclear Safety Agency have reviewed the impact of frequency from smart meters and have stated “Health authorities around the world, including ARPANSA and the World Health Organization (WHO), have examined the scientific evidence regarding possible health effects from smart meters. Current research indicates that there are no established health effects from the low exposure to the RF EME from smart meters”. You can read more here.
Who has access to my electricity usage data?

Data from your electricity meter is considered private information and as such is covered by our Privacy Policy.


How can I find out more information about smart meters?

For more information please refer to relevant State Governments information pages for Qld, SA, NSW and Vic.
